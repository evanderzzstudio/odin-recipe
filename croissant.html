Croissant

omemade croissants are a delightful treat, and making them from scratch can be a rewarding experience. Please note that making croissants is a bit time-consuming, so be prepared for some patience and planning. This recipe yields about 12 croissants.

Greek Yogurt Ingredients

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 1/4 cups warm milk
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold

Steps

  1. Make the Dough: In a small bowl, combine the warm milk and active dry yeast. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture. Mix the ingredients until a dough forms. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Shape the dough into a rectangle, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or until it's chilled and firm.
  2. Prepare the Butter Layer: While the dough is chilling, take the cold butter and place it between two sheets of parchment paper or plastic wrap. Roll it into a 9x12-inch rectangle. Place the butter layer in the refrigerator to chill until firm.
  3. Laminate the Dough: Roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface into a 12x18-inch rectangle. Place the chilled butter layer in the center of the dough. Fold one-third of the dough over the butter layer, then fold the other third on top (like folding a letter). Turn the dough 90 degrees and roll it out into a 12x18-inch rectangle again. Fold the dough in thirds once more. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  4. Repeat the Folding and Chilling: Repeat the rolling and folding process two more times, chilling the dough for 30 minutes between each fold. This creates the layers in the croissants.
  5. Shape and Bake: After the final chilling, roll out the dough to about 1/4-inch thickness. Cut the dough into triangles, then roll each triangle up from the base to form the croissant shape. Place the shaped croissants on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, cover them with a clean kitchen towel, and let them rise at room temperature for about 1-2 hours, or until they have doubled in size.
